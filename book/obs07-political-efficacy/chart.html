Agreement That Officials Don't Care Has Roughly Doubled Since the 1950s

ANES has asked the same question in most of its election-year studies since 1952 — "public officials don't care much what people like me think." Agreement ran as low as a quarter of Americans around 1960; it's run above two-thirds in every reading since 2020.

Reading note: Weighted using ANES's own cross-year weight (VCF0009z); "agree strongly"/"agree somewhat" are combined into one Agree category exactly as ANES harmonizes it for every year shown. "Neither" (1988+) and don't-know/refused responses are excluded from the denominator. Not every ANES study year fielded this item — dots mark the years that did.
