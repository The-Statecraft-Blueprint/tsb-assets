Government Shutdowns Keep Getting Longer

Duration of every major government shutdown since 1995. The pattern is clear: shutdowns are getting longer, more frequent, and more disruptive.

Before 1995: There were 13 short shutdowns between 1981 and 1990, averaging just 2 days each. Most Americans never noticed them. The longest was 3 days. The era of consequential shutdowns begins in 1995.
