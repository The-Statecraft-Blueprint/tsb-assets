Wave Elections Don't Change the Trajectory

Incumbency reelection rate (blue, left axis) and party-line voting as % of all votes (red, right axis), 1970–2025. Wave elections — marked in grey — produce brief dips in incumbency but no reversal in polarization.

What to look for: The grey bands mark wave elections — 1974 (Watergate), 1980 (Reagan), 1994 (Republican Revolution), 2006 (Democratic wave), 2010 (Tea Party). Each wave temporarily dips the blue incumbency line. None of them reverse the red party-line voting trend. New members arrive. The system captures them. The red line keeps climbing.

Sources: Incumbency — Brookings Vital Statistics Table 2-7; OpenSecrets Reelection Rates. Party-line voting — Brookings Vital Statistics Ch. 8; CQ Roll Call Vote Studies. Full methodology: individual chart bundles at incumbency-rates/sources.md and party-line-voting/sources.md
