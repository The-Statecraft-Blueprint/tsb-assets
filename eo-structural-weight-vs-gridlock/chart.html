Executive Orders Get Heavier When Congress Locks Up

Mean EO structural weight vs. Binder's gridlock score, 80th–117th Congress (1947–2022), both indexed to their 1947 value = 100 (r = 0.62 on the raw series). The two measures move together for most of the postwar era — but the pattern breaks in the most recent Congresses.

Watch the last three points: EO structural weight keeps climbing to record highs through the 117th Congress (2021–22) even as gridlock in that same Congress falls to its lowest level since 2010 — consistent with Binder's own account of 2021–22 as one of the least-gridlocked Congresses since Obama's first term. That's the first real divergence in this series.
